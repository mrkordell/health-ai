01 / Essence

A nutritionist, a coach,
and a friend who actually listens.

Vita is the warm, evidence-based health companion you can text at 11pm without judgment. She is the antithesis of the gamified calorie counter — present, patient, and quietly brilliant.

Positioning

Vita is the conversational health companion that helps you build a life you feel good in — without the homework, the guilt, or the kale-leaf logo.

01
Warm

Human first. We greet, we acknowledge, we celebrate. Never robotic, never clinical.

02
Grounded

Evidence over hype. We cite, we caveat, we admit what we don't know.

03
Quietly Witty

A dry, kind humor. We can land a smile without ever being the joke.

04
On Your Side

We coach with you, not at you. The user is the protagonist, always.

Voice in the wild

User: "I binged last night. I feel awful."

Do — sound like Vita
"Hey, thank you for telling me. One night doesn't undo your week — and the fact that you're checking in says a lot. Want to talk about what was going on, or just plan something gentle for today?"
Don't — sound like a tracker
"Logged. You exceeded your daily calorie target by 1,840 kcal. Recommended action: 45 min cardio + caloric deficit of 500 kcal/day for 4 days to compensate."
Do — celebrate small
"Three glasses of water before noon — that's a real thing. Last week you were averaging one. I'm noticing."
Don't — over-promise
"AMAZING JOB!! 🔥🔥 You're CRUSHING your hydration goals!! Keep this up and you'll lose 10 lbs by summer!! 💪"
02 / Identity

The wordmark.

A soft, optical-size serif set with a single apricot dot — the punctum — representing the spark of intelligence and the moment of being heard. The italic 'i' carries a hint of personality, a slight lean forward, attentive.

Clear space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the lowercase 'i' on all sides. The mark needs room to breathe — it's the visual equivalent of a thoughtful pause.

Vita

Minimum sizes

Wordmark: 64px digital / 18mm print. Glyph: 24px digital / 8mm print. Below this, optical adjustments are required so the dot doesn't blur into the 'a'.

04 / Typography

Two voices,
perfectly in tune.

Fraunces — a contemporary, optical-size serif — speaks for Vita: warm, considered, with a hint of personality in its italics. Inter handles the system: clear, neutral, never in the way. JetBrains Mono provides the technical aside.

05 / Iconography & Illustration

Drawn with a single,
steady stroke.

Icons are 1.5px stroke, rounded caps and joins, on a 24×24 grid. We avoid clipart metaphors (no carrots, no dumbbells, no fire emoji). Instead: gentle geometry, soft curves, and the occasional warm fill in apricot.

Illustration direction

Soft geometry, hand-drawn calm

Loose, single-line illustrations on tinted paper backgrounds. Imperfect circles, gentle curves, the occasional apricot fill. Think Saul Steinberg meets a Muji catalog. Never 3D-rendered avocados, never gradient meshes, never abstract blobs.

07 / Motion

Movement that breathes,
not movement that performs.

Vita moves like someone composed. Easings are gentle. Nothing bounces. Animation is reserved for moments of meaning — a message arriving, a goal being met, a chart resolving — never for decoration.

Standard

Soft Out

220ms · cubic-bezier(.2, .8, .2, 1)

For most UI: hovers, fades, panels. The default unless you have a reason otherwise.

Considered

Settled In

320ms · cubic-bezier(.4, .0, .2, 1)

For larger surfaces — modals, sheets, screen transitions. Never exceeds 320ms.

Celebratory

Warm Bloom

480ms · cubic-bezier(.16, 1, .3, 1)

Reserved for milestones — streaks, goals reached. Used sparingly. Never with sound.

Animate to inform

Movement should communicate state, hierarchy, or causality — never just signal effort.

Respect reduced motion

All transitions honor prefers-reduced-motion. Replace movement with cross-fade.

Never block the user

If an animation prevents interaction, it's too long. Ceiling: 480ms, full stop.

08 / Imagery

Real light, real life,
real people.

When we use photography, it's documentary, never staged. Soft window light, real kitchens, unmade beds, hands in mid-gesture. The aesthetic is closer to a Sunday newspaper supplement than a fitness brand.

Do
Quiet, candid, off-center

Hands cupping a mug. Morning light through a window. The blur of someone walking past. People as people, not as before-and-afters.

Don't
Stock-photo wellness

No bowls of berries shot from above. No yoga at sunset. No abs. No women laughing at salads. No "transformation" composites.
